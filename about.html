About

A Concord paint shop with a long memory and a warranty to back it.

Earl Scheib of Concord has been under new ownership since 2010. The name is familiar — the original Earl Scheib paint chain ran TV ads for half a century — but this shop is locally owned and runs on its own standards.

When the new ownership took over, the goal was simple: keep the prices that built the brand's reputation, but raise the bar on what comes out of the bay. That meant better materials, real prep on every job, and warranties customers can actually rely on.

Most customers who shop around find our estimates come in $500 or more below other shops — but that doesn't come from cutting corners on preparation or finish. It comes from a production-line workflow that keeps cycle times tight (most jobs go out in 4–5 days) and from a team that's been doing this work for years.

What we stand for

01

Prep is the job

A paint job lives or dies on what happens before the gun touches the panel. Sanding, cleaning, dust removal, seal coat — not optional.

02

Honest pricing

Free in-person estimates so the price reflects your actual car — not a phone-quoted guess. Three tiers so you only pay for what you need.

03

Insurance, no theatre

We work with every major insurance company, deal with adjusters directly, and offer deductible assistance. You keep driving; we handle the paperwork.

A note on the name

If you remember Earl Scheib commercials, you probably remember the price — "I'll paint any car for $99.95!" That ad campaign ran for decades and built one of the best-known names in American auto paint.

The original chain wound down in 2010. Our shop on Market St kept the location, kept the spirit (affordable paint, fair deals, no nonsense), and rebuilt under independent local ownership. Same address, same trade — updated standards.
