Car care

After your paint job: how to make it last.

A new finish is most vulnerable in the first six weeks. Here's how to protect it — and the products we recommend ourselves.

First six weeks

Wait four to six weeks after your paint job before applying any wax. The clearcoat needs time to fully cure — waxing too soon traps solvents underneath and dulls the finish.

Waxing routine

After the cure period, wax your car every 3–4 months to keep the finish protected. Use an automotive wax that does not contain abrasive cleaning agents like silicone — abrasives are for restoring older finishes, not new ones.

Technique

  1. Wash and dry the car thoroughly first.
  2. Apply a thin coat of wax with the included applicator using short, circular motions.
  3. Cover the entire car panel by panel.
  4. Let the wax dry completely — it should haze over.
  5. Use a clean terry cloth towel to wipe the wax off, again with short circular motions and firm pressure.
